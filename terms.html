3. Sprint Protocol — Service Terms

Race-gated eligibility: The Sprint Protocol is a race-gated service. You must have a confirmed race date between 28 and 56 days from the date of sign-up. This window ensures sufficient time for protocol delivery, training adaptation, and product testing before race day.

Service agreement: All Sprint customers are required to complete and sign a service agreement as part of the intake form before protocol work begins. This agreement outlines the scope of service, athlete responsibilities, and program timeline.

Intake form requirement: Sprint Protocol customers must complete the intake form within 7 days of purchase. Protocol delivery cannot begin until the intake form is submitted. We are not responsible for delays caused by failure to complete the intake form promptly.

Protocol delivery timeline: We target delivery of your personalized protocol within 48 hours of receiving your completed intake form. Delivery times may vary. Protocols are delivered via email.

Weekly revisions: Sprint includes weekly protocol revisions from intake through race day. Revisions are based on your training feedback and adaptation data. The revision loop continues until your race is complete.

Hard rule — no new products within 4 weeks of race day. RIK protocols adhere to a strict rule: no new nutrition products are introduced within 4 weeks of your event. This rule protects your race. The 28-day minimum enrollment window is designed to ensure adequate testing time.

Protocol blocking conditions: For your safety, we do not provide personalized protocols to athletes with the following conditions without clearance from a licensed healthcare provider:

  • Active gastrointestinal conditions (IBS, Crohn's, ulcerative colitis, or similar)
  • Known caffeine sensitivity or cardiac arrhythmia (Euphoria contains 100mg caffeine per serving)
  • History of exercise-associated hyponatremia

If any of these apply, please disclose them in your intake form. We may request medical clearance before delivering your protocol.
